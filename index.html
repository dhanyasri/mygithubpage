McMaster iGEM

McMaster iGEM website

iGEM stands for international genetically engineered machine. It is a synthetic biology competition that began at MIT and now includes over 200 teams. I am part of the McMaster team as the co-head of the wet lab.

The project we are working on this year is engineering a lactobacillus (gut bacteria) strain to target GI tract cancers through immunotherapy. Our goal is to create lactobacilli with an anti-HER2 DARPin that will attach to tumours and release IL-2 through a quorum sensing mechanism.

Immunology Research

I am a student researcher with Dr. Bowdish who is part of the McMaster Immunology Research Centre (MIRC). I am currently looking for sex-based differences in antimicrobial peptide expression in the upper respiratory tract of mice.
